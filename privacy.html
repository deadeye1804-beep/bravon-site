Scope

This policy explains how we process personal data when you visit bravon.it.com, use forms, or interact with us. We process data in line with the GDPR and applicable national law.

Data we process

  • Server logs (IP address, date/time, URLs, user-agent) to maintain security and stability.
  • Form submissions (e.g., name, email, company, instructions) when you submit a demo or contact form.
  • Analytics — we use Plausible Analytics (EU-hosted, cookie-free, privacy-friendly) for aggregate stats.
  • Payments (when applicable) are processed via Stripe. Stripe may process identifiers and payment data.

Purposes & legal bases

  • Operating the website & security — Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interests).
  • Responding to requests / demo configuration — Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (pre-contractual / contract) or 6(1)(f).
  • Analytics (Plausible) — Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interests, no cookies / no cross-site tracking).
  • Payments (Stripe) — Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (contract) and Art. 6(1)(c) (legal obligations like bookkeeping).
  • Marketing emails (if you opt in) — Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent). You can withdraw consent anytime.

Processors & recipients

  • Netlify, Inc. — hosting, DNS, and form handling (submissions stored on Netlify).
  • Plausible Insights OÜ — privacy-friendly web analytics (EU).
  • Stripe, Inc. and its affiliates — payment processing (if/when you purchase).
  • Other vendors we may engage under GDPR Art. 28 DPAs as needed.

Transfers outside the EEA (e.g., to the US) rely on adequacy decisions or Standard Contractual Clauses.

Retention

We keep server logs up to [[30]] days; form submissions up to [[24 months]] or as required to provide services; billing records per statutory obligations (usually 6–10 years).

Your rights

You have the right to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, and to object to processing. You can lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority. Contact: privacy@bravon.it.com.

Cookies & tracking

We don’t use marketing cookies. Plausible is cookie-free. Embedded third-party content (if any) may set their own cookies — such content is loaded only when necessary.
